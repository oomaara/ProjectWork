Architecture that I like in Rīga

Tallinas street 23

"A small rental house, this building stands as a notable example of early ornamental Art Nouveau style. Construction began in 1901 based on the designs of architect Konstantins Pēkshens (Konstantīns Pēkšēns), featuring a blend of traditional eclectic elements like pilasters and sandriks. During construction, Eugene Laube (Eižens Laube), then an architecture student, extensively transformed the narrow facade, characterized by its three windows on each floor. The composition emphasizes color and texture contrast, utilizing various materials such as red brick, light or dark plaster, metal, and natural slate."

*These photos and description were taken from Jūgendstils

The facade of Tallinas iela 23, also known as the Pēkšēns House, features several symbolic elements typical of Art Nouveau architecture:

  • Floral Motifs
  • Masks and Faces
  • Curved Lines and Whiplash Motifs
  • Mythological and Symbolic Creature
